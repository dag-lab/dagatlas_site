Labialization

Labialization is a secondary articulation involving the lips while producing another sound. This map describes 27 languages of East Caucasian language family.

There are seven languages of the sample lacking labialized vowels (25.93%). The rest of the languages in the sample demonstrates labialized consonants. Since the number of the components varies they were divided into three groups.

The first group is a group of languages with a small number of labialized consonants attested – up to 10 phonemes. These languages are marked as «small» on the map. Second group is a group with an average number of labialized consonants attested – from 13 to 26 phonemes. These languages are marked as «medium» on the map. Second group includes 9 languages. Five language of the sample demonstrate relatively large amount of labialized consonants – from 34 to 46 phonemes. These language form a third group of languages and marked as «large» on the map.
